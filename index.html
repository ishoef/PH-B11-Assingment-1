Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a visionary economist and social entrepreneur, is celebrated globally for his groundbreaking work in alleviating poverty and empowering marginalized communities. Born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, Dr. Yunus has dedicated his life to fostering financial inclusion and social equity. Dr. Yunus is best known as the founder of the Grameen Bank, established in 1983. His journey began in the 1970s when he encountered the dire financial struggles of rural women in Bangladesh. Traditional banking systems excluded the poor, leaving them trapped in cycles of poverty. Recognizing this gap, Dr. Yunus pioneered the concept of microfinance—providing small, collateral-free loans to empower individuals, particularly women, to start small businesses and achieve financial independence.
